# 🚲 Bike Sales Performance Analysis


## 📊 Project Overview
This project presents an in-depth analysis of global bike sales performance, leveraging transactional data spanning from 2011 to 2016 across five key international markets. The interactive dashboard was designed to convert complex, raw datasets into clear and meaningful business intelligence, with a strong emphasis on revenue growth trajectories, cost efficiency, quarterly performance breakdowns, and customer demographic insights.
The primary objective of this analysis is to equip business stakeholders and decision-makers with the visibility needed to identify high-revenue markets, assess profitability by region and age group, track cost patterns over time, and uncover strategic opportunities for market expansion and customer targeting.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
- Microsoft Power BI; Dashboard design and interactive data visualization
- Power Query; Data transformation, cleaning, and preparation
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions); Custom measures, KPI calculations, and advanced analytical logic

  
## 💡 Data Insights
- Revenue Growth Trend; Total revenue grew consistently from $9.0M in 2011, reaching its peak at $20.0M in 2015 before experiencing a modest decline to $17.7M in 2016, suggesting strong market expansion during the mid-period with early indicators of market saturation toward the latter years.
- Sustained Profitability; The business maintained a robust profit margin of 37.79% across all recorded periods, reflecting disciplined cost management and operational efficiency relative to a total revenue of $85,271,008 and a total cost of $53,049,908.
- Geographic Revenue Concentration; The United States emerged as the dominant market, generating $28M in revenue and $11M in profit, considerably outpacing Australia ($21M), the United Kingdom ($11M), Germany ($9M), and France ($8M), indicating a strategic opportunity to further diversify revenue across underperforming international markets.
- Customer Age Segmentation; Adults represent the highest-value demographic, contributing $43M in revenue, followed by Young Adults at $31M and Youth at $12M. The near-zero contribution from the Seniors segment highlights a largely untapped customer base that could be explored through targeted product and marketing strategies.
